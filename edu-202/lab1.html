Lab 1: Environment Setup

LAB 1

Scenario: You are preparing to configure Zscaler services for your organisation. Before diving into policy configuration, you must verify your Skytap virtual lab environment is running and confirm you can sign into the Zscaler Experience Center console.

Time: ~10 minutes
Environment: Skytap + Zscaler Experience Center
💭 THINK FIRST · LAB SETUP

A few minutes of orientation before you configure anything prevents costly mistakes. Think about what the Zscaler Experience Center is and why your Student FQDN matters across every lab.

  • The Zscaler Experience Center combines ZIA, ZPA, and ZDX administration under one URL. Why is unified management significant for an engineer versus managing three separate consoles?
    Q1 — MODEL ANSWER
    Unified management means policy correlation is visible in a single pane — you can trace a traffic flow from ZCC client status through ZIA security policy into ZPA private access without switching consoles. For an engineer this is critical during troubleshooting: a ZPA access failure might actually be a ZIA SSL inspection issue, and you need both views simultaneously. It also means changes to identity (ZIdentity) propagate to all services from one place.
  • Your Student FQDN (e.g. zs000149.labtenant.com) appears in usernames, app segment FQDNs, and external URLs throughout every lab. What would go wrong if two students accidentally used the same FQDN?
    Q2 — MODEL ANSWER
    Both students' App Connectors would enrol into the same tenant, their App Segments would share FQDNs, and policy changes by one student would affect the other's tests. External URLs for Browser Access would conflict at the DNS/certificate level. The FQDN is your unique namespace — every object you create is scoped to it, ensuring lab isolation.
  • The Skytap pod contains multiple VMs (Client PC, App Connectors, Windows Server). What is the risk of not starting all VMs before beginning configuration tasks?
    Q3 — MODEL ANSWER
    App Connector VMs that are offline cannot register with ZPA, so any App Segment configured against them will show "No healthy connectors" and access will fail. The Windows Server is needed for DNS entries (Lab 6) and RDP targets (Lab 5). Starting all VMs upfront ensures each service is available when the lab step requires it, and avoids confusing "policy is correct but traffic fails" symptoms caused by infrastructure being offline.
Task 1.1 Start the Skytap Lab Environment ▾
  1. Using the Skytap access URL from your Lab Access Instructions, open the Skytap environment in your browser.
  2. In the Skytap environment view, select all VMs in the pod.
  3. Click the Play button to start all virtual machines simultaneously.
    Skytap pod — check the top-level checkbox to select all VMs, then click Play. Corp: Client PC 1, Docker Server, Log Streaming Connector and the remaining VMs will all start together.
  4. Wait for all VMs to reach Running state before proceeding.

Note: Starting all VMs together ensures App Connectors are online before you configure ZPA objects in later labs.

Task 1.2 Sign In to the Experience Center and Explore the Dashboard ▾
  1. From the Skytap portal, open Corp: Client PC 1 in a browser window. Click the Keys icon in the Skytap toolbar and select Insert Credentials to sign into Windows automatically.
    Corp: Client PC 1 — the Windows VM opens in a browser tab. Use the Skytap toolbar to insert credentials.
    Keys icon (padlock) in the Skytap toolbar — click the insert arrow next to student to auto-fill the Windows login.
  2. Once on the Client PC desktop, open a browser and navigate to console.zscaler.com.
  3. Enter your Student Admin credentials:
    • Username: student@<Student FQDN>
    • Password: <Session Password>
    ZIdentity sign-in — console.zscaler.com redirects here. Enter student@<Student FQDN>, your session password, then click Sign In.
  4. Once signed in, explore the Experience Center (EC) dashboard. Note the top navigation: Analytics, Administration, Policies, Infrastructure, and Logs.
    Experience Center — unified console at console.zscaler.com. The five top-nav menus span ZIA, ZPA, ZDX, and ZIdentity administration.
  5. Under Administration, observe the unified management options including ZIA, ZPA, ZDX, and ZIdentity configuration. Use the Search icon (or Ctrl+K) to quickly jump to any feature — you'll use this constantly throughout the labs.
    Search menu (Ctrl+K) — type any feature name to navigate instantly. Recent searches are shown for quick access.

Note: The Experience Center is the single pane of glass for all Zscaler service administration. Your Student FQDN and password are used throughout all labs — save them in the Lab Configuration panel above.
